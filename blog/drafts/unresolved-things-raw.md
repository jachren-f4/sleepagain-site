# Raw Draft — Unresolved Things and Sleep

In the book, I talk about having a week of really bad sleep, terminal insomnia, waking up early. Then, after a week of that, I took a glass of whiskey, and I slept more hours than in many nights. I even felt this kind of snap in my mind after the first sip of whiskey went down and got digested. So, in a matter of minutes, I felt the buzzing of the fight or flight stress nervous system turn off and go into quiet and this kind of easy mode because of the alcohol.

I now actually had a similar experience, which isn't in the book because it just happened this weekend, where I had a lot of issues with a very close person to me. We had some unresolved things that we were not really feeling that there was anything necessarily going on, but it was obvious that both of us needed to talk through what we had in our minds. I had had, again, this kind of really bad week of not sleeping well. We had a call to talk things through, and it wasn't a call to do that. It was just a regular catch-up. What ended up happening is we really talked about the things that were running through our heads, and it felt like the call then went into a lot of casual stuff and really good spirits and everything.

Then I went to the supermarket, and I was just walking in the supermarket, and I felt this kind of similar switch went on or off in my head where the nervous system stopped overreacting like it did back in the thing I mentioned in the book. So here, the same kind of trigger was that there was some unresolved thing that my nervous system was keeping things elevated because of these unresolved things. When they got resolved during that call, it was like the danger was gone. There was no danger anymore. I could rest again. So the fight or flight response didn't have any meaning left.

I think there are a lot of these kind of, how do you resolve things? The journaling, writing, this kind of brain dump before you go to bed, kind of getting resolutions done, I think is very helpful for people's health, like better sleep. You can do a lot more if you resolve challenging things. It's not about just, oh, just for the sake of it, but it can be a really big trigger for insomnia when you have unresolved things that you're subconsciously or consciously thinking about. You can have a relief moment by confronting things in the open, on paper, talking to somebody, taking action.
